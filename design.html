Introduction

Heatwaves are among the most impactful extreme weather events. Accurate prediction is important for public safety, energy demand, and climate risk management.

However, numerical weather prediction is highly sensitive to initial conditions, meaning small errors in the initial atmospheric state can grow into large forecast errors.

Key Insight: Data assimilation can significantly improve model predictions by combining observations with model forecasts.

Data Assimilation Background

Data Assimilation Concept

Data assimilation combines model background information with observations to produce a more accurate atmospheric state called the analysis.

⚡ analysis = background + Kalman gain × innovation

Data Definition

  • Background: ERA5 model estimate
  • Observation: ASOS measured temperature
  • Innovation: observation − background
  • Analysis: DA-corrected temperature
Kalman Gain Equation
K = PbHT(HPbHT + R)-1

Pb = Background Error Covariance | H = Observation Operator | R = Observation Error Covariance

Conclusion

  • Regional Performance: Data assimilation improved temperature estimates in both regions, but GW required stronger corrections due to larger background errors.
  • Time & Spatial Comparison: DA reduced RMSE in both time and spatial comparison experiments.
  • Observation Density Impact: Observation density and regional characteristics affected correction magnitude.
  • Gangwon Characteristics: Showed larger correction, likely due to sparse observations and complex terrain.
  • Practical Significance: The experiment demonstrates the practical role of DA in heatwave prediction.
🔍 Key Insight

This research demonstrates how observation density and regional characteristics critically influence data assimilation performance. For extreme weather prediction like heatwaves, the accuracy of initial conditions is paramount, and data assimilation significantly improves model forecasts.
